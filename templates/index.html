Caretaker vs. Child Utterances

This visualization presents the results of a regression model applied to a portion of the CHILDES corpus. Each utterance was labeled as coming from either a caretaker or a child, while utterances from investigators were excluded from the analysis. The purpose of this exploration was to see whether it is possible to predict the speaker based solely on the characteristics of the utterance.

The model has some clear limitations, but certain patterns do emerge. Longer and more grammatically complete phrases are typically attributed to the caretaker, while shorter or grammatically incorrect phrases are more often associated with the child. For example, a phrase like “it go there” is likely predicted as coming from a child, whereas “it goes there” is attributed to the caretaker. A similar pattern appears with word choices such as “mommy” versus “mom.”
